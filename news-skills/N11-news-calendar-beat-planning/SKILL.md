# N11: News Content Calendar & Beat Planning

## Objective
Build a predictable, proactive news publishing schedule. Instead of reacting to news as it happens, maintain a forward-looking calendar that tracks known announcements, policy review dates, budget seasons, and travel patterns. This ensures consistent publishing volume and prevents dead days.

## News Beat Categories

### Beat 1: Scheduled Announcements (Predictable)
These happen on known dates and can be planned weeks in advance.

| Event | Frequency | Typical Timing | Preparation |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **UK Immigration Rule Changes** | Quarterly (Apr, Jul, Oct, Jan) | First week of the month | Draft article 1 week before |
| **Canada IRCC Policy Updates** | Monthly | Varies, check IRCC website | Monitor IRCC newsroom |
| **Schengen Visa Fee Review** | Annual | January | Prepare comparison table |
| **US Visa Bulletin** | Monthly | 2nd week of month | Draft analysis |
| **Australia Migration Program** | Annual (July) | Budget release day | Pre-write, publish same day |
| **India Budget (Union Budget)** | Annual (Feb) | Budget day | Pre-write travel/visa impact |
| **EU Entry/Exit System (EES)** | Milestone-based | Track official EU announcements | Have evergreen explainer ready |
| **UK Graduate Route Review** | Periodic | Announcement expected mid-2026 | Monitor official channels |

### Beat 2: Seasonal Patterns (Anticipatable)
These follow predictable seasonal trends.

| Season | Months | Visa Topics | Content Angle |
|--------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Summer Rush** | Apr-Jun | Student visas, tourist visas | "Apply now — processing times increase" |
| **Fall Intake** | Jul-Sep | Student visas, post-arrival | "Last minute tips for September intake" |
| **Holiday Travel** | Oct-Dec | Tourist visas, Schengen, US | "Holiday visa timeline — apply by [date]" |
| **New Year** | Jan-Mar | All visa types | "2026 visa outlook — what's changing" |
| **Graduation Season** | May-Jun | Work visas, PR pathways | "From student visa to PR — your options" |
| **Budget Travel** | Sep-Oct | Tourist visas, visa-free travel | "Best budget-friendly destinations for Indians" |

### Beat 3: Reactive Coverage (Unpredictable)
These cannot be scheduled but must be covered when they happen.

| Trigger | Response Time | Template |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Embassy closure/opening | Within 4 hours | N3 Type 4 |
| Sudden policy reversal | Within 2 hours | N3 Type 1 |
| Natural disaster affecting visas | Within 4 hours | N3 Type 4 |
| Bilateral agreement announcement | Within 24 hours | N3 Type 2 |
| Visa fee waiver/promotion | Within 24 hours | N3 Type 3 |
| Scam alert / fraud warning | Within 12 hours | Special alert format |

## Monthly Planning Process

### Step 1: Known Events (Start of Month)
```
□ List all known announcements expected this month
□ Add bank holidays (India + target countries — processing delays)
□ Mark visa application deadlines (e.g., "apply by X for fall intake")
□ Flag any expiring programs/policies
□ Identify seasonal content opportunities
```

### Step 2: Content Slots (Weekly)
```
□ Reserve 3-4 "reactive" slots per week (for breaking news)
□ Reserve 1 "planned" slot per day (for known events/analysis)
□ Reserve 1 "roundup" slot per week (Friday)
□ Reserve 1 "buffer" slot per week (Saturday — catch-up)

Total minimum: 5 articles/week
Target: 7-10 articles/week during busy periods
```

### Step 3: Source Check (Daily)
```
□ Check all T1 source feeds (embassies, immigration depts)
□ Check Google News alerts
□ Check social media for breaking news
□ Update calendar with any new items found
```

## Publishing Volume Targets

| Period | Min Articles/Week | Target Articles/Week | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Normal | 5 | 7 | Standard news flow |
| Busy season (Apr-Jun, Sep-Oct) | 7 | 10-12 | Student visa + holiday rush |
| Slow season (Dec, monsoon) | 3 | 5 | Focus on analysis/guides |
| Major event week (budget, policy overhaul) | 10 | 15+ | Publish as fast as news breaks |

## Content Mix Targets

| Content Type | % of Output | Purpose |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Breaking news (🔴) | 15% | Urgent, immediate-impact stories |
| Standard news (🟡) | 25% | Regular policy updates |
| Analysis / explainer | 20% | "What this means" deep dives |
| Roundup / digest | 15% | Consolidate minor updates |
| Guide updates | 15% | Keep evergreen content fresh |
| Seasonal / preview | 10% | Forward-looking content |

## Holiday & Blackout Calendar

Track days when news is unlikely or when publishing should pause.

| Date | Event | Impact on News |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Indian holidays** | Diwali, Holi, Eid, etc. | Reduced readership — schedule around these |
| **Country holidays** | UK bank holidays, Canada Day, etc. | No official announcements likely |
| **Weekends** | Sat-Sun | Fewer official announcements; good for roundups |
| **Budget days** | India Budget (Feb), UK Budget (Mar) | Major announcement potential |
| **Year-end** | Dec 24-Jan 2 | Slow news period — plan year-ahead content |

## News Source Monitoring Schedule

| Time | Channels to Check |
|------|-------------------|
| **Morning (9:00 IST)** | T1 source feeds, Google News alerts, Twitter lists |
| **Midday (13:00 IST)** | Check for afternoon announcements (common in EU/UK) |
| **Evening (17:00 IST)** | US/Canada announcements (morning their time) |
| **Night (22:00 IST)** | Late-breaking stories, prepare for next morning |

## 🧠 Daily "Find News" Workflow Integration

When you say **"find news"** (N0 orchestrator), this runs at the START and END:

```
At START of daily session:
1. I check today's calendar slot (reactive vs. planned)
2. I check for known events today (budget, policy review, etc.)
3. I check if there are overflow/buffer items from yesterday

At END of daily session:
4. I log today's articles in the calendar
5. I mark which calendar slots were filled
6. I update the weekly publishing count
7. I preview tomorrow's known events
8. I report: "You're at 4/7 articles this week — 3 more to go"
```

**Time spent in daily workflow:** ~3 min (start) + ~3 min (end)

## Output
- Weekly content calendar with beats and assignments
- Monthly known-events tracker
- Holiday blackout calendar
- Publishing volume tracker (actual vs. target)
- Content mix report (breaking vs. planned vs. roundup)

## Quality Check
- [ ] Weekly calendar created by Sunday evening for the coming week
- [ ] At least 5 articles published per week (minimum)
- [ ] No more than 2 consecutive days without published content
- [ ] Content mix targets within ±10% of goals
- [ ] Known events added to calendar at least 1 week in advance
- [ ] Holiday blackouts accounted for in scheduling
- [ ] Source monitoring done at least twice daily
- [ ] Buffer slots used for catch-up when needed
- [ ] Weekly digest published every Friday (or last working day of week)
